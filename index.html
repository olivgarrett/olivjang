I'm a reporter with an interest in writing, data visualization and engagement. In December 2019, I'll be graduating from the University of Missouri with a dual-degree in investigative journalism and political science and a minor in Spanish.

I'm currently a designer on the graphics desk of the Columbia Missourian. I've spent most of my time over the past few years in the Missourian newsroom, with stints as a reporter, member of the community outreach team and assistant city editor. Along the way, I've also worked with PolitiFact Missouri, POLITICO Europe and my campus newspaper, the Maneater.

I built this portfolio website using HTML, CSS and a bit of JavaScript.

These are a few of the projects I'm most proud of:

Tolton Catholic's namesake progresses on a path to sainthood While driving across the state and searching through historical records, I had to figure out how to put to words the complicated relationship between race and religion.

Documentary recalls 30-year-old fight for wetlands wastewater treatment at Eagle Bluffs Reporting this story was a breath of fresh air, complete with punny names, protest folk songs and waterfowl. I was able to highlight how an innovative and environmentally-conscious wastewater treatment system came to be.

Unbound Book fest ripped for lack of inclusion, silencing panelist last year I had to grapple with ethical questions and sources who at first wanted to stay off the record for this breaking news story about allegations of racism at a festival that is a source of pride in the community.

New Historical Society building will connect Missourians to a rich past I toured my first construction site and took advantage of the chance to slow down and choose my words carefully.
