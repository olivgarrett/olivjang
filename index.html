I'm a reporter with an interest in local journalism. My work has included regular coverage of schools and cities, service journalism and longform pieces about funerals, dinosaurs, saints and knitters. I'm currently a general assignment reporter for the Dubuque Telegraph Herald in Dubuque, Iowa. I graduated from the University of Missouri in 2019. As a student, I spent time in the Columbia Missourian, Politifact Missouri, POLITICO Europe and Maneater newsrooms.

Originally from Texas, school and work took me to Missouri and then Iowa. I've picked up plenty of reporting skills, but I'm still learning how to handle the cold weather. In my free time, I frequent book stores, parks and hiking trails where I can bring my cat, the farmers market and knitting circles.

Local officials offer tips on coping with grief during holidays

Breaking News

Mineral Point rocked after two firefighters die in crash

Dubuque Telegraph Herald, Jan. 7, 2022

When two small-town firefighters were killed in a deadly crash, I was sent to write the first story for the Telegraph Herald. At the time I was writing, the names of the deceased had not yet been released, but already the news was sending shockwaves around the region. I was tasked with documenting how the community was reacting in the immediate aftermath of the loss, though information and hard facts were still unknown.

City of Dubuque letter outlines 'grave concerns' about railway merger, requests overpass, other support

Dubuque Telegraph Herald, Jan. 1, 2022

As the only reporter working on the Friday before New Years Day, I was holding down the fort and covering any story that came in. This was a fairly simple story about local concerns regarding a railroad merger. I've decided to highlight it because I was proud of the number of people I was able to reach on relatively short notice on a holiday when sources were tricky to round up.


Longform

Final say: Funeral practices offer options for deceased, bereaved

Dubuque Telegraph Herald, Oct. 24, 2021

Generally speaking, front page stories at the TH are 20-25 inches and inside stories are 15 inches. But, each Sunday, a reporter is given the chance to write a 60-80 inch centerpiece about any topic they'd like. This story idea came to me in a dream. Over the course of several weeks, I interviewed people about death, grief, funerals and what it's like to work in the funeral and other funeral-adjacent industries.

Behind the scenes on riverboat cruises: Area ships a source of fun, excitement for passengers

Dubuque Telegraph Herald, Aug. 29, 2021

An editor asked me to write a feature for the business page and, seeing the opportunity to hop on a river boat, I jumped on it. This is the type of story I enjoy doing -- something where I get ask all my burning questions about something fun that I know nothing about.
